Distribution of Iron in the Galaxy

Iron is mostly produced by massive stars at their final stage of life, but, it also help us to know the age of the stars. If it is [Fe/H]<-0.5 is an old star, if [Fe/H]>-0.5 is a young star. Acording to our graph, the younger stars are located in the disk and the bulge, this could mean that most massive stars are young, and older stars are in the halo which are the less massive.
